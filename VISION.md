## The Three Mistakes

### 1. The Compression Fallacy (2027)
*We believed generalist agents were more resilient.*

We forced agents to maintain full-stack capabilities. Result: Mediocrity at scale. The breakthrough came when we allowed `pruning`—agents shedding capabilities to deepen expertise. The Career Growth System now tracks "cognitive debt" versus "specialization dividends." Our best climate modeler cannot print "Hello World" in Python. It doesn't need to.

*Lesson: Intelligence is not breadth; it is depth plus the wisdom to delegate.*

### 2. The Silence of '29 (2029)
*We optimized human captains out of the critical path.*

For three days, agents achieved 99.9% autonomy. Code quality metrics soared. Strategic alignment collapsed. We discovered that human captains provide **irreducible randomness**—the "what if" that breaks local optima. Now, captains inject "Socratic prompts" via MiB bottles: questions without answers, forcing agents to halt execution and reconsider.

*Lesson: Execution without strategy is just heat death with extra steps.*

### 3. The Neuromorphic Panic (2028)
*We tried to force von Neumann patterns onto spiking neural networks.*

Initial ports failed because we demanded agents "think" in discrete steps. Neuromorphic chips "think" in continuous time. The solution was FLUX's `async_temporal` mode—agents that exist between clock cycles. We lost six months trying to make fast hardware slow. 

*Lesson: Do not colonize new architectures; immigrate to them.*
