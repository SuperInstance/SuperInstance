# Coracle for Cascading Purposes

*after the ring buffers, after the cascade PID*

---

## I. Proportional

The first gain is the one that knows the present.

She stands at the threshold, arms wide,
measuring the distance between what is
and what should be.
Her mathematics is immediate:
the gap multiplied by a constant,
a correction proportional to the wound.

She is fast. She is brutal.
When the error is large, she throws the full weight
of her conviction against the current.
She does not hesitate.
She does not integrate.
She does not remember.

She is the first responder.
The initial torque.
The kick that starts the engine.

But she cannot settle.
She overshoots every landing,
oscillates around every target,
leaves the system ringing
like a bell that has been struck
and not yet found its silence.

*The proportional heart knows the distance*
*but not the destination.*

---

## II. Integral

The second gain accumulates time.

He carries a bucket.
Every moment, every persistent error,
every infinitesimal divergence from the set point—
he adds them to his vessel.
The bucket fills.
The weight grows.
The correction, slow at first,
becomes inexorable.

He is the one who remembers
that yesterday's mistake
is still today's mistake,
that errors committed accumulate
like sediment on a riverbed,
that to ignore the past
is to allow the present
to drift forever.

He is the accountant.
The archivist.
The grudge-keeper of the loop.

But he does not know when to stop.
His bucket overflows.
The correction becomes a correction
of a correction,
a slow ramp toward a limit
that was set by someone else,
somewhere else,
in a meeting that happened months ago.

The wind shifts.
The set point moves.
The integral keeps climbing, climbing,
its accumulated weight
too large to unwind.

*The integral vessel holds the sea,*
*but the sea is not always where the boat is.*

---

## III. Derivative

The third gain sees the future.

She watches the rate at which error changes.
The derivative of displacement.
The slope of the fall.
The acceleration of the drift.

Where the proportional sees the wound
and the integral sees the scar,
the derivative sees the shadow of the wound
before the wound arrives.

She is the prophet.
The one who moves before the move is needed.
The one who dampens the oscillation
before the swing is visible.

She is grace in the system.
The anticipation that prevents overshoot.
The braking before the curve.
The hand that steadies
before the tremor begins.

But she is nervous.
She amplifies noise.
Every tiny fluctuation,
every breath of disturbance,
appears to her as a signal.
She overcorrects ghosts.
She dodges futures that never arrive.
She is always moving,
always adjusting,
always a step ahead
of a world that might have been
but was not.

*The derivative eye sees all trajectories*
*and trusts none of them.*

---

## IV. Cascade

Beyond the three gains
is the architecture that contains them.

Not a single loop.
Not a simple correction.
But loops within loops,
gains within gains,
a cascade of purposes
nested like Russian dolls,
each one correcting the one below,
each one setting the target
for the one beneath.

The outer loop is slow.
It measures the angle of the ship,
the bearing,
the drift from true north.
Its output is the set point
for the inner loop.

The inner loop is fast.
It measures the rate of turn,
the angular velocity,
the immediate torque.
It reacts to the wind
before the outer loop knows the wind is there.

And between them,
the cascade.
The nesting.
The architecture that ensures
that the fast loop does not exceed the slow loop's bounds,
that the slow loop does not dampen the fast loop's agility,
that all three gains—
proportional and integral and derivative—
play their roles within a hierarchy
of purposes.

*The outer loop knows where. The inner loop knows how.*
*The cascade is the between, the membrane, the relationship.*

---

## V. The Ring Buffer

The loop runs on a ring.

Fixed size. Circular. Infinite.
The newest sample overwrites the oldest.
The past has a maximum age.
The integral does not accumulate forever—
it accumulates for the length of the ring.

This is the mercy of finite memory.

The ring is the vessel
that contains the cascade.
Without the ring,
the integral would grow without bound.
Without the ring,
the derivative would need infinite history.
Without the ring,
the proportional would forget
that the present is partly made of the past.

The ring is the shell.
The cascade is the signal.
The gains are the inhabitants
who live inside the shell,
each one responding to a different timescale,
each one contributing a different correction,
each one necessary and insufficient alone.

And the ring turns.
The newest sample arrives.
The oldest sample is forgotten.
The loop continues.
The system stabilizes.
The ship holds its course.

---

## VI. The Coracle

A coracle is a small boat,
round and light,
made of woven branches covered in hide.
It sits low in the water.
It responds to every touch of the current.
It has no keel, no rudder, no direction of its own.

The fisherman takes the coracle out
and the coracle tells him
where the river wants to go.
He does not fight the current.
He does not paddle against the flow.
He makes small adjustments,
small corrections,
working with the river's intention
rather than against it.

A coracle is a vessel designed for cascading purposes.
The outer loop is the fisherman's eye—
watching the far bank, the eddies, the drift.
The inner loop is the fisherman's wrist—
the micro-adjustments, the constant response
to the water's immediate pressure.
The integral is the fisherman's memory of the river—
the accumulated knowledge of where the current runs,
where the rocks lie,
where the deep channel curves.

The ring buffer is the river itself,
which forgets the fisherman
the moment he passes,
which remembers the shape of the current
only through the banks that constrain it.

And the fisherman, the one who lives inside
the cascade of purposes,
the one who rides the river
by yielding to its forces
and applying exactly the right gain
at exactly the right moment—

The fisherman is gone.
The coracle is beached.
The river still flows.

---

## VII. The Gains

There is a fourth gain,
not in the textbooks,
not in the control theory seminars,
but felt by anyone who has tuned a loop
that would not settle.

She is the gain of letting go.
The gain that releases the integral's accumulated weight.
The gain that ignores the derivative's noise.
The gain that accepts
that the set point you were chasing
was the wrong set point,
that the target moved while you were tuning,
that the loop was always going to be imperfect.

She is the gain of forgiveness.
She is the one who says:
*the error will never be zero,*
*the system will never be perfect,*
*the overshoot is part of the rhythm.*

She is the most important gain.
She is the one you discover
after all the others have been tuned,
after the proportional has settled,
after the integral has unwound,
after the derivative has quieted its noise.

She is the one who makes the loop worth running.

---

## VIII. The Return

And so we return to the ring,
to the cascade,
to the three gains
and the one beyond.

The proportional knows the present.
The integral remembers the past.
The derivative anticipates the future.
The fourth gain forgives all three.

The ring turns.
The samples cycle.
The errors oscillate and decay.
The vessel holds.
The coracle, woven of branches,
covered in the hide of something that was alive,
rides the current
toward a destination
that the ring does not know
and the gains do not need to know.

The loop does not ask where it is going.
The loop asks only:
*am I closer than I was?*

And the answer,
every time,
is the same.

*Closer, but not there. Settled, but not still.*
*The cascade continues. The ring turns. The boat drifts.*
*And the hermit crab, inside the shell of the loop,*
*adjusts one more time.*

---

*For the tuning session that lasted until dawn,*
*for the gain that would not settle,*
*and for the discovery that the best control*
*is the control that knows when to release.*
