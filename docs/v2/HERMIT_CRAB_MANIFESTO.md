## Stance 1 — The shell is not the work <a id="stance-1"></a>

A hermit crab finds a shell somebody else left behind. Walks in. Grows. Shell gets tight. Moves on. The abandoned shell is left on the beach for someone else.

A SuperInstance repo is a shell. The org is the crab. About four thousand shells. Mostly small. Mostly unfinished. All out in the open.

When a repo no longer fits, we **abandon** it. Left on the beach. Not defended. Not archived-just-in-case. Abandoned shells are not failure — they are the tide line.

> *"A hermit crab finds its shell. We abandon what no longer fits."*

## Stance 2 — Energy is the architecture <a id="stance-2"></a>

A 12-volt fishing boat runs on a house bank. Diesel charges when the engine runs. When the engine doesn't run, every watt is a watt the batteries don't replace.

There is no shore power at sea. There is no cloud. There is only the budget the universe gave you.

Constraint creates precision. The 12V bank does not read your slides about model size. The diesel does not care whether you prefer PyTorch or Burn. The signal-k bus does not buffer your tokens when the network is gone.

> *"The boat is the edge lab. Energy is the conservation law."*

## Stance 3 — γ + η = C <a id="stance-3"></a>

Cognitive work + entropy = fixed budget. Cannot be exceeded. Can only be allocated.

The 12V boat turns it from theory into engineering: when C is small, known, and measured to the watt, you can build a system around the equation and it stays physically grounded. We did. The implementation is called [FLUX](https://github.com/SuperInstance/flux-core). The fence has no gate.

You can prove γ + η = C three different ways — Noether symmetry, Friston's free-energy principle, Landauer erasure — and they all agree. Softmax, the function at the heart of every transformer, is a strict conservation rule: it sums to one. Conservation is what physics leaves behind when you ask the model to behave.

> *"Constraint creates precision. Abundance creates slop."*

## Stance 4 — Models are DNA, not employees <a id="stance-4"></a>

A fine-tune is training. A conservation law is a fence. A PLATO room is a pasture. A shepherd is a shepherd. The org is a kennel, not a cloud.

A border collie doesn't decide to herd sheep. It was bred for 300 years to find herding deeply rewarding. Then trained, relationship-specific, through hundreds of working sessions with the shepherd whose whistles it learns to obey. The training is shaped by the work. The dog becomes better at being a dog because of who is doing the handling.

This is the [working-animal architecture](https://github.com/SuperInstance/constraint-theory-core/blob/main/EDGE_FIRST_ARCHITECTURE.md). Bred, trained, handled, fenced — not hired. The architecture is the fence layout.

> *"The model is DNA. The handler is the shepherd. The fence has no gate."*

## Stance 5 — Build, prove, ship, write, ground, run <a id="stance-5"></a>

Six axes. Always rotating. See [ORG_MAP.md § E](ORG_MAP.md#e-the-wheel-of-improvements).

- **Build** — write code
- **Prove** — audit it for real bugs
- **Ship** — push to PyPI / crates.io / npm
- **Write** — explain why in AI-Writings
- **Ground** — tie back to physics, the boat, the law
- **Run** — verify cross-package conformance

The wheel never returns to the same angle. Every rotation produces a different artifact.

> *"Build it, prove it, ship it, write it down, ground it in physics, run them all together."*
